# Rabies Vaccination Coverage Heterogeneity

Power mean models quantifying how spatial heterogeneity in vaccination coverage affects rabies transmission.

## Citation

Ferguson EA, Lugelo A, Czupryna A, Anderson D, Lankester F, Sikana L, Dushoff J, Hampson K (2025). Improved effectiveness of vaccination campaigns against rabies by reducing spatial heterogeneity in coverage. *PLOS Biology* 23(1): e3002872.

## Links

- **Paper**: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.3002872
- **GitHub**: https://github.com/ElaineAFerguson/Serengeti_vaccination_heterogeneity
- **Zenodo**: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.15249730

## Metadata

- **PPL**: brms (baseline) + Stan (custom power mean models)
- **Language**: R
- **Published**: May 2025
- **Scale**: 87 villages x 252 months, 21 years of campaign data, 12 Stan model variants
